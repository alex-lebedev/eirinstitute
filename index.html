Is It Burnout or Just Fatigue?

You wake up tired — even after sleeping. When rest no longer restores you, it's not laziness — your system has likely been running in overdrive for too long. This isn't a motivation problem, it's depletion.

Loss of interest in things you once enjoyed

Hobbies, projects, or people that used to light you up now feel flat or distant. That numbness is a conservation mode — your mind saving energy.

Small tasks feel overwhelming

Emails, messages, even planning dinner feels "too much." Your cognitive load is maxed out; pushing harder won't fix it — relief and pacing will.

Your body is signaling

Headaches, tight chest, shallow breathing, or a constant hum of tension. Your nervous system is trying to tell you something.

Emotional flatness

You feel disconnected from joy, from depth, from the things that used to matter. Life feels muted, like you're watching from behind glass.

What Science Tells Us

Research on depression, smoking cessation, and burnout:

After 2 guided psilocybin sessions (MDD): 71% had ≥50% symptom reduction at 4 weeks; 54% were in remission.

Ref: Davis AK, et al. JAMA Psychiatry 2020. PubMed

1-year durability (same cohort): ~75% maintained response; ~58% remained in remission at 12 months.

Ref: Gukasyan N, et al. J Psychopharmacol 2022. PubMed | PMC

5-year follow-up (same cohort): 67% were in remission at ~5 years after treatment.

Ref: Davis AK, et al. Journal of Psychedelic Studies 2025. DOI | Press Release

Smoking cessation (long-term): 60% biologically verified abstinent at long-term follow-up (mean 30 months; range 16–57 months) after psilocybin-assisted program.

Ref: Johnson MW, et al. J Psychopharmacol 2017. PubMed | PMC

Burnout & depression in frontline clinicians (DB-RCT): At day 28, depression scores improved −21.3 with psilocybin vs −9.3 with niacin (Δ ≈ −12, p<.001); burnout measures trended better.

Ref: Back AL, et al. JAMA Network Open 2024. PubMed | PMC

Burnout/depression—group model: Psilocybin-assisted group therapy + MBSR outperformed MBSR alone on depressive symptoms (no serious AEs).

Ref: Lewis BR, et al. JAMA Network Open 2025. PubMed | PMC
